<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

**The United States Forces excel at combined arms warfare** with versatile infantry, powerful armor options, and devastating artillery support. American doctrines emphasize flexibility, allowing players to adapt to any battlefield situation while leveraging superior firepower and late-game scaling.

From elite airborne assaults to overwhelming armored advances, USA commanders have access to six distinct doctrines that cover every tactical approach—defensive attrition, aggressive breakthrough, precision anti-tank warfare, and mobile superiority.

</div>

<Callout variant="tip">
**USA Strengths:** Elite infantry options, strong medium armor, excellent artillery support, and powerful off-map abilities make USA a versatile faction capable of adapting to any opponent.
</Callout>

<div class="my-12 border-t-2 border-primary/30"></div>

## Infantry Company

<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

**Classic defensive doctrine built for attrition warfare.** Hold ground with cost-effective infantry and overwhelming artillery, grinding opponents down through superior firepower and staying power.

</div>

### Specialty Units & Signature Tools

<div class="p-6 rounded-lg border-2 border-primary/20 bg-card/30 mb-6">

**29th Infantry Division Riflemen** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Inexpensive line infantry</span> that can merge into depleted squads and weapon teams to sustain defensive positions

**1st Special Service Force (Devil's Brigade)** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Elite commando call-in</span> for shock actions and emergency breakthrough operations

**Robust Indirect Fire** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Mortars, howitzers, and rockets</span> for sustained bombardment from safe positions

</div>

### Strategic Summary

<div class="space-y-4 mb-6">

Infantry Company is the ultimate turtle doctrine. Establish defensive positions with cheap, numerous infantry supported by powerful team weapons, then pulverize attacking forces with abundant artillery. The merge mechanic keeps weapon teams operational even under heavy fire, while Devil's Brigade provides elite shock troops when you need to break through or counter-push.

</div>

### Tactical Profile

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 mb-8">

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Strengths**

- Sturdy defensive infantry with merge capability
- Outstanding indirect fire to suppress and destroy from range
- Resilient attrition economy—can outlast most opponents
- Elite Devil's Brigade available for critical moments

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Weaknesses**

- Poor mobility and offensive capability
- Limited mobile AT beyond base roster
- Heavy armor can be problematic without support
- Munitions-dependent for barrages and upgrades

</div>

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50 mb-8">

**Meta Classification:** <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Infantry • Defensive • Artillery</span>

**Best Against:** Aggressive infantry-heavy builds, early push strategies

**Struggles Against:** Heavy armor concentrations, mobile harassment tactics

</div>

<div class="my-12 border-t-2 border-primary/30"></div>

## Tank Destroyer Company

<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

**Reactive anti-armor specialist.** Counter enemy armor with infantry AT, dedicated tank destroyers, and devastating off-map artillery that punishes armored concentrations.

</div>

### Specialty Units & Signature Tools

<div class="p-6 rounded-lg border-2 border-primary/20 bg-card/30 mb-6">

**Enhanced Infantry AT** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Bazookas and AT grenades</span> give infantry early anti-armor capability

**M36 Jackson** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Long-range tank destroyer</span> for ranged armor duels and kiting tactics

**Powerful Off-Map Artillery** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Devastating barrages</span> to break up armored thrusts and punish grouped vehicles

</div>

### Strategic Summary

<div class="space-y-4 mb-6">

Tank Destroyer Company is purpose-built to counter armor-heavy strategies. Equip your infantry with AT weapons early, then transition into dedicated TDs like the M36 Jackson for long-range penetration. Use off-map artillery to force enemy armor to disperse or retreat, preventing concentrated pushes.

</div>

### Tactical Profile

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 mb-8">

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Strengths**

- Efficient infantry AT from the start of the match
- Strong dedicated TDs for ranged armor engagements
- Exceptional off-map support disrupts enemy formations
- Excellent counter-pick against armor doctrines

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Weaknesses**

- Specialized vehicles struggle vs infantry and structures
- Late-game penetration vs super-heavies unreliable
- No extra artillery units—limited siege potential
- One-dimensional gameplay focused on AT

</div>

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50 mb-8">

**Meta Classification:** <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Anti-Tank • Off-Map • Reactive</span>

**Best Against:** Heavy armor doctrines, tank-focused strategies

**Struggles Against:** Infantry swarms, defensive emplacement builds

</div>

<div class="my-12 border-t-2 border-primary/30"></div>

## Ranger Company

<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

**Elite infantry supported by assault armor.** Dominate infantry engagements with US Army Rangers, then breakthrough with durable Jumbo Shermans backed by heavy artillery.

</div>

### Specialty Units & Signature Tools

<div class="p-6 rounded-lg border-2 border-primary/20 bg-card/30 mb-6">

**US Army Rangers** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Elite all-purpose infantry</span> for superior combat effectiveness throughout the match

**M4A3E2 Jumbo Sherman** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Heavy assault tank</span> with exceptional armor to spearhead pushes

**Strong Indirect Fire** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Mobile and static artillery</span> to break fortifications and suppress defenders

</div>

### Strategic Summary

<div class="space-y-4 mb-6">

Ranger Company leverages elite infantry superiority to win engagements throughout the match, then transitions into durable assault armor for breakthrough operations. Rangers provide consistent battlefield presence while Jumbos absorb damage and lead assaults. Heavy artillery support enables both offensive breakthroughs and defensive stands.

</div>

### Tactical Profile

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 mb-8">

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Strengths**

- Top-tier infantry from mid-game onward
- Durable Jumbos can spearhead aggressive pushes
- Potent artillery for both offense and defense
- Strong combined arms synergy

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Weaknesses**

- Munitions-hungry (Ranger upgrades plus barrages)
- No super-heavy AT beyond TDs or upgraded Shermans
- Rangers are expensive—manpower bleeds hurt
- Jumbos lack penetration against heavy armor

</div>

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50 mb-8">

**Meta Classification:** <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Elite Infantry • Assault • Artillery</span>

**Best Against:** Standard infantry builds, mixed compositions

**Struggles Against:** Heavy tank destroyer builds, super-heavy armor

</div>

<div class="my-12 border-t-2 border-primary/30"></div>

## Airborne Company

<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

**Elite paratroopers with air superiority.** Seize early map control with 101st Airborne, then maintain dominance through pack howitzers and devastating air strikes.

</div>

### Specialty Units & Signature Tools

<div class="p-6 rounded-lg border-2 border-primary/20 bg-card/30 mb-6">

**101st Airborne Paratroopers** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Available from game start</span> for immediate elite infantry pressure

**M1A1 75mm Pack Howitzer** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Rapid infantry suppression</span> from a mobile, paradropped platform

**M10 Wolverine** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Light tank destroyer</span> for mobile armor control

**Late Air Support** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">P-47 strikes</span> and other aircraft for precision damage

</div>

### Strategic Summary

<div class="space-y-4 mb-6">

Airborne Company is all about early aggression and map control. Deploy elite paratroopers immediately to secure key positions, then support them with paradropped pack howitzers that can suppress and damage enemy infantry. Transition into lighter TDs for armor control while leveraging powerful air strikes in the late game to eliminate key targets.

</div>

### Tactical Profile

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 mb-8">

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Strengths**

- Unmatched early infantry pressure
- Great support weapons for pinning enemy forces
- Versatile air strikes in late game
- Excellent map control and mobility

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Weaknesses**

- Manpower bleed from expensive elite units
- Late game struggles vs heavy armor
- High munitions demand for air power
- Falls off if early advantage isn't capitalized

</div>

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50 mb-8">

**Meta Classification:** <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Airborne • Elite Infantry • Early Pressure</span>

**Best Against:** Slow-building strategies, defensive doctrines

**Struggles Against:** Cost-efficient infantry masses, heavy armor focus

</div>

<div class="my-12 border-t-2 border-primary/30"></div>

## Mechanized Company

<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

**Vehicle-centric mobility doctrine.** Dominate map control with capping light vehicles, then transition into diverse medium armor backed by mobile fire support.

</div>

### Specialty Units & Signature Tools

<div class="p-6 rounded-lg border-2 border-primary/20 bg-card/30 mb-6">

**Extensive Light Vehicles** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">All can capture sectors</span> to turbocharge map control and resource acquisition

**Broad Medium Tank Roster** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Including M4A3E8 "Easy Eight"</span> and multiple Sherman variants for tactical flexibility

**Mobile Indirect Fire** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">On-the-move fire support</span> to back up armored thrusts without stopping

</div>

### Strategic Summary

<div class="space-y-4 mb-6">

Mechanized Company snowballs map control through capping light vehicles that secure resources faster than any other doctrine. Leverage this economic advantage to field a diverse fleet of medium tanks tailored to each engagement, supported by mobile artillery that keeps pace with your advances.

</div>

### Tactical Profile

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 mb-8">

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Strengths**

- Unparalleled vehicle variety (especially mediums)
- Light vehicles capture to accelerate resource gain
- Good mobile fire support for coordinated pushes
- Flexible composition adapts to threats

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Weaknesses**

- Fuel-intensive to maintain vehicle mass
- Micro-heavy in mid/late with diverse fleet
- Struggles vs super-heavies without support
- No heavy armor or elite infantry options

</div>

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50 mb-8">

**Meta Classification:** <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Mechanized • Mobility • Medium Armor</span>

**Best Against:** Slow, infantry-focused builds, static defenses

**Struggles Against:** Heavy tank destroyer ambushes, super-heavy armor

</div>

<div class="my-12 border-t-2 border-primary/30"></div>

## Armored Company

<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

**Brute-force armor doctrine.** Build a cost-efficient tank force with discounted Shermans, then anchor it with the M26 Pershing heavy tank for late-game dominance.

</div>

### Specialty Units & Signature Tools

<div class="p-6 rounded-lg border-2 border-primary/20 bg-card/30 mb-6">

**M26 Pershing Heavy Tank** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">True heavy armor</span> for late-game armor duels and breakthrough operations

**Discounted Medium Tanks** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Cost-efficient Shermans</span> enable larger armored forces earlier in the match

**3rd Infantry Division Riflemen** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Improved mainline infantry</span> provide better support for your armor

**"Acceptable Losses" (15 CP)** — <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Replenish destroyed tanks</span> during a critical window for decisive assaults

</div>

### Strategic Summary

<div class="space-y-4 mb-6">

Armored Company focuses on overwhelming armor superiority through improved tank economics. Build cheaper medium tanks to establish armor dominance, then transition to the Pershing heavy tank as your anchor. The unique "Acceptable Losses" ability allows you to replace destroyed tanks during crucial moments, enabling all-in armor assaults without fear of irreplaceable losses.

</div>

### Tactical Profile

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 mb-8">

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Strengths**

- Improved tank economy enables larger armored forces
- Access to true heavy (Pershing) for late armor duels
- Upgraded mainline infantry provides better support
- "Acceptable Losses" enables aggressive late-game plays

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50">

**Weaknesses**

- Reduced light vehicle variety and weaker early game
- Minimal artillery beyond basic mortars
- Still fuel-hungry despite discounts
- Pershing is costly and risky to lose

</div>

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded-lg border border-border bg-card/50 mb-8">

**Meta Classification:** <span class="text-fs-gold font-semibold">Armor • Medium Mass • Pershing</span>

**Best Against:** Infantry-heavy builds, lighter armor compositions

**Struggles Against:** Heavy tank destroyer builds, artillery-focused doctrines

</div>

<Callout variant="success">
**Pro Tip:** The "Acceptable Losses" ability is game-changing for final pushes. Time it with your most aggressive armor assault to replace casualties on-the-fly, maintaining momentum when it matters most.
</Callout>

## USA Doctrine Quick Reference

<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

Not sure which doctrine fits your playstyle? Here's a quick breakdown:
