## <Target className="inline-block mr-3 mb-1" size={32} /> Assault Rifles ‚Äî Range Dominance

<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

Assault rifles are the undisputed kings of 30-50m engagements. Most ARs maintain a **5-shot kill** at 50m regardless of close-range performance, creating exceptional consistency. High-damage ARs that 3-shot at point-blank still require 5 shots at distance, while standard ARs need the same 5‚Äîeffectively equalizing the class at range. This predictable damage retention makes ARs the most reliable choice for medium-long combat.

The key differentiator becomes fire rate: faster-firing ARs deliver their 5 shots quicker, achieving better TTK. However, even slower ARs remain competitive, typically ranging from 0.29-0.40s at 50m‚Äîfar superior to SMGs or light carbines at those distances.

## <TrendingDown className="inline-block mr-3 mb-1" size={32} /> Submachine Guns ‚Äî The Drop-Off Disaster

<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

SMGs suffer the most dramatic performance collapse of any automatic weapon class. Where SMGs excel at 0-20m with 4-5 shot kills and 0.17-0.28s TTK, they become **7-8 shot kills** at 50m‚Äîeffectively doubling their required hits. Damage drop-off from ~25 down to ~14 means SMGs need nearly twice as long to kill at distance.

This isn't a minor handicap‚Äîit's a fundamental role limiter. At 50m, even the fastest SMGs take 0.44-0.50s to kill, losing decisively to ARs (0.29-0.40s) and LMGs. The sweet spot for SMGs ends at approximately 30m; beyond that, you're at a severe disadvantage against any rifle-caliber weapon.

## <Shield className="inline-block mr-3 mb-1" size={32} /> Light Machine Guns ‚Äî Range Supremacy

<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

LMGs dominate long-range combat with exceptional damage retention. Several heavy LMGs maintain **4-shot kills at 50m+**‚Äîbetter than assault rifles‚Äîwhile lighter LMGs match AR performance at 5 shots. Even 5-shot LMGs like the L110 and DRS-IAR kill as fast as the best ARs (0.30-0.35s), while the 4-shot models achieve elite 0.36-0.44s TTK at 50m.

## Final Insights

<div class="space-y-6 mb-8">

Long-range TTK analysis reveals that **damage retention is king**. Weapons maintaining high per-shot damage at distance require fewer bullets, achieving better TTK despite identical or slower fire rates. This is why the M240L (4-shot at 50m) beats most ARs despite lower ROF, and why SMGs collapse at range despite having the fastest close-range TTK.

Three critical factors determine long-range performance: **damage drop-off profile, rate of fire, and shots-to-kill**. Weapons with gentle drop-off curves (LMGs, heavy carbines) keep low shot counts. High ROF partially compensates for extra shots needed. And weapons requiring 7-8+ bullets at range become uncompetitive regardless of fire rate.
